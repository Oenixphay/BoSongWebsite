What to See

  • Chestnut Hill Historic District
    This picturesque neighborhood is known for its charming Victorian-era homes and tree-lined streets. Wander through the district to admire the beautifully preserved architecture.

  • Woodmere Art Museum
    A small but lovely museum showcasing local artists and historical art collections, located in a grand historic mansion.

  • Wissahickon Valley Park:
    While not strictly in Chestnut Hill, this large park is nearby and offers scenic trails, waterfalls, and natural beauty, making it a perfect place for hiking and outdoor activities.

What to Do

  • Shopping along Germantown Avenue
    Chestnut Hill's main street is lined with independent boutiques, antique shops, and local galleries. It's perfect for a leisurely shopping experience.

  • Take a Walk in the Wissahickon
    Explore the wooded trails of the Wissahickon Gorge, which is known for its tranquility, beautiful streams, and historic bridges.

  • Visit the Morris Arboretum
    A large, beautiful garden with an impressive collection of plants, walking paths, and a canopy walk with views of the surrounding forest.

What to Eat

  • Chestnut Hill Brewing Company
    A cozy local brewery offering craft beers and a pub-style menu with options like burgers, pizza, and pretzels.

  • Bredenbeck's Bakery
    A beloved spot for baked goods, especially famous for its traditional doughnuts, cookies, and cakes.
