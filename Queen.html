What to See

Click for more info!

  • Betsy Ross House
    The home of Betsy Ross, the woman credited with making the first American flag. Learn about her life and the history of the American Revolution.

  • Mario Lanza Park
    A small, charming park with a statue dedicated to famous tenor Mario Lanza. It's a nice spot to relax in the heart of Queen Village.

  • The Fabric Workshop and Museum
    A contemporary museum focused on art, textiles, and design, with rotating exhibitions and interactive installations.

What to Do

Click for more info!

  • Visit South Street
    Known for its quirky shops, restaurants, and vibrant nightlife, South Street is the heart of Queen Village's local culture.

  • Walk Along the Delaware River
    Take a stroll along the river at the waterfront or enjoy outdoor seating at one of the many riverfront restaurants.

  • Explore the Antique Shops
    Queen Village is known for its antique stores, offering everything from vintage furniture to rare collectibles.

What to Eat

Click for more info!

  • Haven
    A contemporary American bistro known for its seasonal menu and inventive dishes like roasted chicken and local vegetables.

  • Miss Rachel's Pantry
    A popular spot for plant-based dishes, offering vegan comfort food and homemade pastries.

  • The South Street Souvlaki
    A beloved Greek eatery serving gyros, souvlaki, and fresh pita, perfect for a quick bite while exploring the neighborhood.
